Values of Unix Culture

Eric S. Raymond, The Art of Unix Programming (1999-2003; esp. Chapter 6: Basics of Unix Philosophy)

Unix Philosophy:
Four Basic Themes

  1. Autonomy: Modularity (1), Composition (3), Separation (4)
  2. Robustness (8), comprised of:
    • Transparency (7): Representation (9), Repair (12)
    • Simplicity (5): Clarity (2), Parsimony (6), Least Surprise (10), Silence (11)
  1. Time: Economy (13), Generation (14), Optimization (15), 17 Extensibility
  2. Diversity (16): ‘Distrust all claims for “one true way”.’

Autonomy: “Unix tradition strongly encourages writing programs that read and write simple, textual, stream-oriented, device-independent formats.”

Robustness: “...software is transparent when you can look at it and immediately see what is going on. It is simple when what is going on is uncomplicated enough for a human brain to reason about all the potential cases without strain.”

Time: “A prematurely optimized portion of a design frequently interferes with changes that would have much higher payoffs across the whole design, so you end up with both inferior performance and excessively complex code.”

Diversity: “multiple languages, open extensible systems, and customization hooks everywhere”
